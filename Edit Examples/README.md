# Persona Edit Categories: Examples

Here we provide examples of the four main categories of edits made to persona items during RQ1 evaluation. The examples are slightly modified for anonymity and to avoid revealing any proprietary information, but they are representative of the types of edits observed in the evaluation.

---

## 1. Completeness

**Definition:** Adding missing context or qualifiers to make items more complete.

### Example 1

**Item:** “Perform PO sanity checks to align information from vendors with market data, ensuring accuracy in the procurement process”  
**Revised to:** “Some supply planners will also act as buyers. In those cases, they perform PO sanity checks to align information from vendors with market data, ensuring accuracy in the procurement process.”

### Example 2

**Item:** “Coordinate with suppliers to resolve delivery issues.”  
**Revised to:** “Coordinate with suppliers to resolve delivery issues, especially when delays affect committed customer orders or production schedules.”

### Example 3

**Item:** “Monitor inventory levels across regions.”  
**Revised to:** “Monitor inventory levels across regions to identify shortages, excess stock, and opportunities for rebalancing supply.”

### Example 4

**Item:** “Collaborate with sales teams on customer needs.”  
**Revised to:** “Collaborate with sales teams to understand customer priorities, urgent orders, and account-specific constraints.”

---

## 2. Recency

**Definition:** Updating outdated or obsolete information, such as renamed tools.

### Example 1

**Item:** “Mastering RapidResponse for demand forecasting.”  
**Revision:** “RapidResponse” was renamed to “Maestro” in 2024.

### Example 2

**Item:** “Using RapidResponse to generate weekly supply plans.”  
**Revised to:** “Using Maestro to generate weekly supply plans.”

### Example 3

**Item:** “Exporting reports from XXX for review.”  
**Revised to:** “Exporting reports from YYY for review.”

### Example 4

**Item:** “Submitting supplier updates through the old procurement portal.”  
**Revised to:** “Submitting supplier updates through the current supplier collaboration portal.”

---

## 3. Precision

**Definition:** Adjusting scope or specificity to improve clarity or accuracy.

### Example 1

**Item:** “Knowledge of regulatory requirements and compliance standards in relevant industries.”  
**Revised to:** “Some knowledge of regulatory requirements and compliance standards in relevant industries.”

### Example 2

**Item:** “Responsible for all supplier communication.”  
**Revised to:** “Responsible for selected supplier communications related to delivery timing, order status, and supply constraints.”

### Example 3

**Item:** “Expert in demand forecasting.”  
**Revised to:** “Familiar with demand forecasting outputs and their implications for supply planning decisions.”

### Example 4

**Item:** “Manages procurement for all products.”  
**Revised to:** “Supports procurement-related planning activities for assigned product categories.”

---

## 4. Redundancy/Logic

**Definition:** Removing duplicative or illogical phrasing.

### Example 1

**Item:** “Supply Planners rely heavily on task flows and structured task flows to manage recurring monthly processes.”  
**Revision:** The phrase “and structured task flows” was removed.

### Example 2

**Item:** “Supply planners review and check planning outputs to verify and validate the results.”  
**Revised to:** “Supply planners review planning outputs to validate the results.”

### Example 3

**Item:** “The planner uses dashboards and visual dashboard views to monitor exceptions.”  
**Revised to:** “The planner uses dashboards to monitor exceptions.”

### Example 4

**Item:** “Supply planners plan future supply plans for upcoming planning cycles.”  
**Revised to:** “Supply planners prepare supply plans for upcoming planning cycles.”
